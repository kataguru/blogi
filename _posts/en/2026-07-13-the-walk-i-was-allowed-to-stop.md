I didn't go on the trip because I was sure of my ability to cope. I left because I had arranged for myself permission to interrupt.

I decided once again to tour Kyrkösjärvi with the dogs. The route is about 16 kilometers long, and it takes three and a half hours to walk briskly. Halfway there is a hut where you can make coffee, eat snacks and feed the dogs.

The route is nice because it cannot be corrected. When you start to go around the lake, you have two options: continue forward or return the same way back. It makes the round clear, but for an everyday run it is on the upper limit. You feel like doing it, but just barely.

This time the state of alertness was not right. I still didn't want to settle the matter in advance by staying at home.

I thought I could ask my wife to pick me up if I ran out of energy. The road runs close to the route in many places, so it would be easy to pick up. I didn't have to commit to the whole round. It was enough to get me going.

## The decision always moved to the next point

The route turned out to be more difficult than usual. The walk was already tiring from the beginning, and the feeling did not improve in the way it sometimes does when the body warms up and a rhythm is found.

I decided to continue to the halfway house. There I could easily judge whether I should ask for a ride.

I took a break at the house, ate lunch and fed the dogs. The rest refreshed me a bit, but didn't make the round easy. I still decided to continue. The idea was simple: I walk as far as I like and then call to pick up.

After a while, irritation came alongside the fatigue. That's usually a helpful signal. When normal stress starts to turn into irritation, the system's capacity has already clearly decreased.

I decided to continue until the power plant. There is a good bench where you could wait for a ride.

I sat at the power plant for a while. Then I thought waiting would be boring. I might as well walk slowly forward and call a little later.

I left to continue.

## The pick-up call was missed

I walked calmly. I no longer had to get there or prove anything. A ride was still possible.

At some point I completely forgot to call.

Then there was so little of the route left that it wouldn't have made sense to pick it up. I walked with the dogs to the end and returned to the starting point.

The journey did not become easy. I was still tired and the tour was not particularly enjoyable. The difference was that I didn't have to carry the whole sixteen kilometer task in my mind.

I had to get home first. Then as far as you liked. After that, to the bench of the power plant. In the end, just a little forward, because waiting in place seemed like a worse option.

The whole journey was made up of a series of small, undoable decisions.

## The exit route reduces the weight of the decision

It's not worth building a story about willpower out of this. If I had been clearly in bad shape, picking up would have been the right solution. The possibility of suspension was not a scam but a real backup route.

That's exactly why it worked.

If I had required myself to commit to the whole round already at home, I probably would have stayed home. The task would have been too big considering the state of alertness at the time. When interruption was allowed, the threshold for leaving decreased.

The same structure applies elsewhere. Hard work does not always become possible by increasing motivation. Often a better way is to reduce the size of the next decision and leave the exit route open.

No need to decide the whole day, project or trip. Just finish the next checkpoint.

After that, the situation will be reassessed.

This time I ended up going around the entire Kyrkösjärvi. Not because I forced myself to finish, but because I was allowed to stop all along the way.
