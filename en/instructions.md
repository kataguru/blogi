# Theory and instructions

This page is for readers who want to understand the model behind the site. The posts themselves are in the archive.

## What is it about?

The human operating system is neither a theory of personality nor a ready-made doctrine of a good life. It is an analysis model based on one's own life.

The idea is simple: a single event can expose the system. A disruption in everyday life, fatigue, mistake, success, dog dental care, the fall of the sauna floor or an artificial intelligence tool is not just a separate story. It can show how resources, friction, feedback, environment and decisions affect each other.

## How should texts be read?

Read the texts primarily as stories.

After them you can ask:

- which part of the system does this observation apply to?
- what burdened you?
- what guided the activity?
- what was missed?
- which small repair shop changed or could change the situation?

## What does operating system mean?

In a computer, the operating system allocates resources, handles disturbances, keeps background processes running, limits rights and recovers from errors.

In humans, the corresponding layer can be seen in how energy is enough or runs out. How the body gives signals. How pleasure is calibrated. How thinking gets locked. How the environment guides choices. How social boundaries protect or leak. How does a person regain direction when the system has drifted too far from its own purpose.

## What does the site not claim?

The site does not claim that the same model works for everyone.

The argument is more limited: this is how the system can be seen in the author's own life. If you recognize the same structure in yours, the template may be useful.
